My Research Interests and Focus Areas

Mathematical Biology

Ecosystems

This research field focuses on mathematical modeling in ecology, with particular interest in how species interactions influence the long-time behavior of ecosystems. Using differential equations and dynamical systems theory, I study questions related to coexistence, extinction, stability, and transitions between different ecological states. These models provide a mathematical framework for understanding how mechanisms such as predation, competition, and resource limitation shape population dynamics and ecosystem persistence.

Keywords: Lotka-Volterra model, trophic functions, predation and competitive interactions.

Epidemiology

In this area, we have two main viewpoints:
(i) the between-host transmission dynamics, which can tell us whether the disease will die out or become endemic. Sometimes, we can also investigate a good control strategy by analyzing the model.
(ii) the within-host dynamics, which can help us understand how the virus interacts with the host's immune system, and how this interaction affects the progression of the disease and the effectiveness of treatments.

Flow Free Problems

Given an n-by-n checkerboard and k pairs of colored dots in some blocks. The flow free problem asks whether we can find k paths on the checkerboard satisfying the following conditions:
(i) each path connects a pair of same colored dots;
(ii) each path only passes through blocks that are not occupied by other paths;
(iii) all blocks on the checkerboard are occupied by some path.
This problem is NP-complete, and it is interesting to investigate some special algorithms to find the solution.
